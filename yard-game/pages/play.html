How to Play

Learn in 60 seconds, play all night.

Quick Start

Three steps. No manual. Just grab the discs and go.

1

Set Up

Place the LED targets on level ground, space them out, and power on the lights. The glow zones activate instantly - you will know when it is ready.

2

Choose Teams

Pair up for 2v2 or go free-for-all. Frisbeam works with any group size, so nobody sits on the sideline. More players, more energy.

3

Throw

Aim for the glow zones and let it fly. When your disc connects with a lit target, you will see it and hear it. That is the moment everyone plays for.

Scoring

Simple math, big drama. Here is how every point counts.

Beam Shot = 3 Points A direct hit on the center target - the beam zone - scores three points. This is the money throw. When you nail it, the whole field knows.
Outer Glow Ring = 1 Point Hit the outer glow ring and you pick up one point. It keeps you in the game and sets up your next big play. Consistency wins rounds.
Cancellation Scoring After each round, subtract the lower team's score from the higher team's score. Only the difference goes on the scoreboard. If Team A scores 5 and Team B scores 3, Team A gets 2 points that round. Every throw matters because your opponent can erase your lead.
First to 21 Wins The first team to reach exactly 21 points takes the game. House rule: you must win by 2 to keep things competitive. Ties just mean more time under the lights.

Pro Tips

Four throws every champion has in their back pocket.

Distance

The Arc

Loft the disc on a high arc to cover distance and drop it right onto the target from above. The key is a smooth, upward release angle with steady spin. Best used when you are throwing from the far end and need accuracy over power.

Precision

The Skip

Release the disc low and flat so it skims in on a line drive. A tight, controlled throw that stays predictable all the way to the target. This is your bread-and-butter when you need a reliable point and the wind is working against you.

Advanced

The Fade

Put a deliberate curve on your throw to bend the disc around obstacles or defenders. Angle your wrist at release and let the spin do the work. When it curls into the beam zone from an impossible angle, it is the most satisfying shot in the game.

Strategy

The Power Play

Go aggressive early. Stack beam shots in the first half to build a lead, then force your opponents into risky throws trying to catch up. Momentum wins games - put the pressure on before they find their rhythm.

Tournament Format

Ready to take it to the next level? Here is how to run a proper bracket.

A Frisbeam tournament uses a double-elimination bracket. Every team gets two chances - lose once and you drop to the losers bracket. Lose twice and you are out. Each match is best 2 out of 3 games, so comebacks happen all the time.

Grab at least 4 teams (8 or 16 is ideal), seed them randomly or by signup order, and let the brackets do the rest. One evening under the glow, one champion, and plenty of stories to tell afterward.

How many teams do I need?

You can run a tournament with as few as 4 teams, but 8 or 16 makes for the best bracket. Odd numbers work too - just give the top seed a first-round bye.

How long does a tournament take?

For an 8-team double-elimination bracket, plan on about 3 to 4 hours. Each game takes roughly 15 to 20 minutes. Add in breaks and you have got a full evening of competition.

What if a game ties at 21?

Keep playing. In tournament play, you must win by 2 points. Rounds continue until one team pulls ahead by 2 or more. It makes for some incredible finishes under the lights.

Do I need multiple Frisbeam sets?

One set works for a small tournament, but two sets let you run matches on parallel fields and cut the total time in half. The Pro Set is recommended for competitive play.

How do I seed the bracket?

For a casual tournament, random seeding keeps things fair and fun. If you have played before, seed by past performance or do a single qualifying round to set the rankings.

House Rules

Official rules are great. These are better.

Night Mode

Kill every light except the LED targets. No porch lights, no phone flashlights, nothing. The only thing you can see is the glow zones, and the only thing guiding your throw is instinct. It completely changes the feel of the game - throws you thought you had dialed in suddenly become a challenge all over again. This is Frisbeam the way it was meant to be played.

Speed Round

Set a 30-second shot clock for each player's turn. Once the timer starts, you throw as fast as you can. No time to overthink - just pure instinct. Use a phone timer and watch the pressure build. The crowd gets loud, the throws get wild, and the scores get unpredictable.

Survivor

Play with 3 or more teams. After every round, the team with the lowest score is eliminated. Keep going until one team is left standing. It is fast, it is ruthless, and it is perfect for larger groups who want high stakes without long waits.

Team Swap

After every game, partners shuffle and form new teams. You will end up playing with everyone and against everyone. Great for parties where not everyone knows each other - by the end of the night, they will. It keeps the energy fresh and the rivalries unpredictable.

Got the Rules Down?
