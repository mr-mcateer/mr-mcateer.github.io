Brand Essence

"Light up the night." Frisbeam exists to extend social moments past sunset with technology that feels like magic. Every decision -- from product design to Instagram captions -- should reinforce this single idea: when the sun goes down, the fun keeps going.

The brand sits at the intersection of outdoor culture, nightlife energy, and accessible tech. Frisbeam is not a gadget company. It is a social catalyst that happens to glow.

Typography

Frisbeam uses three typefaces. Space Grotesk brings geometric, techy energy to headlines. DM Sans provides clean readability for body text. JetBrains Mono handles data and code references.

Display: Space Grotesk

Light Up the Night

Built for Every Gathering

Space Grotesk 700 (headlines) / Space Grotesk 600 (section headers, nav, buttons, prices)

  • Character: Geometric, techy, modern. Slightly futuristic without being cold.
  • Weights: 700 (Bold) for hero headlines. 600 (Semi Bold) for section headers, navigation, buttons, and prices.
  • Usage rule: Never use more than two sizes of Space Grotesk on a single page.
  • Letter spacing: Slightly tightened (-0.01em to -0.03em) at display sizes for a premium feel.

Body: DM Sans

Bold labels and emphasis within body copy.

Medium weight for subheadings and inline emphasis.

Regular weight for paragraphs, descriptions, and long-form content with generous line height.

DM Sans 700 (bold) / 500 (medium) / 400 (body text)

  • Character: Clean, approachable, excellent readability. Warm without being playful.
  • Line height: 1.6-1.7 for body paragraphs. 1.3-1.4 for UI elements.
  • Max line length: 65-75 characters per line for readability. Enforce with max-width on text containers.

Voice & Tone

Frisbeam's brand voice is "the friend who throws the best party." Warm, confident, never corporate. The voice stays constant across all channels. The tone adjusts to context.

Voice Principles

  • Short sentences. If it has more than 20 words, split it. Rhythm matters more than completeness.
  • Active voice. "Frisbeam lights up your night" -- not "Your night is lit up by Frisbeam."
  • Specific details. "Aircraft-grade aluminum frame" -- not "premium materials."
  • Humor that lands naturally. Funny because it is true, not because it is trying to be funny.

Words We Use

  • "You" and "your" -- the reader is the main character, not the brand.
  • "Get," "buy," "grab" -- direct language people actually use.
  • Action verbs in headlines -- Light, Throw, Score, Gather, Build, Play.

Words We Never Use

  • "Revolutionary" -- earned only by products that actually change industries. A disc game is not one of them.
  • "Game-changing" -- the irony is not worth the cringe.
  • "Purchase," "utilize," "leverage" -- corporate speak that repels real people.
  • "Consumers" -- say "people," "players," or "you."
  • "Innovative," "synergy," "ecosystem" -- banned. Permanently.
  • Exclamation points everywhere -- one per page maximum. Earn it.

Photography Direction

Photography is the most powerful brand signal after the logo. Every image should answer the question: "What does it feel like to play Frisbeam at night?" The answer is always warmth, laughter, friendly competition, and glow.

Always

  • Real people, natural expressions. Candid moments during actual gameplay. The best shots happen between poses.
  • Nighttime or golden hour. This is the core context. The game is designed for darkness. Show it in its element.
  • Warm lighting. Color grade toward amber and golden tones. The palette should feel like a summer evening turning into night.
  • Group shots. Frisbeam is a social game. Minimum two players in every hero image. Groups of 4-8 are the sweet spot.
  • Diverse representation. Mixed ages (25-55 primary), mixed groups, real body types. Aspirational but relatable.

Never

  • Stock photo poses. No looking-at-camera-while-laughing, no handshake photos, no thumbs up. These destroy credibility instantly.
  • Overly polished. No studio setups, no controlled lighting rigs. Studio kills the outdoor energy.
  • Daytime-only. Showing Frisbeam only in daylight misses the entire point. The glow IS the experience.
  • Solo product on white background. Product-only shots are for Amazon listings, not for brand storytelling. Always show context.

The Glow Aesthetic

LED light should be visible but not overwhelming. The people are the subject. The glow is the atmosphere. Think campfire energy -- the light draws everyone together, but the faces tell the story. Never let the LED wash out the human moment.

Logo Usage

The Frisbeam "F" icon uses the gradient-brand (cyan to purple). It is the single most recognizable element of the identity.

F

Frisbeam

Gradient icon + Space Grotesk wordmark. Dark backgrounds preferred.

  • Gradient: The "F" icon always uses gradient-brand (cyan to purple, 135deg). Never flatten to a single color.
  • Clear space: Minimum clear space around the icon equals the height of the icon itself.
  • Background: Dark backgrounds are preferred. On light backgrounds, use the solid primary (#00D4FF) version.
  • Minimum size: 24px width for digital. 10mm for print.
  • Never: Rotate, stretch, add drop shadows, change gradient colors, or place on busy photography without a dark overlay.

Do / Don't Examples

These examples demonstrate the difference between Frisbeam voice and generic marketing copy. When in doubt, read it out loud. If it sounds like something a real person would say around a fire pit, it passes.

Do "Frisbeam turns any night into an event."
Don't "Frisbeam is a REVOLUTIONARY game that will CHANGE your life!!!"
Do "Sets up in 30 seconds. Battery lasts all night."
Don't "Our proprietary quick-deploy system leverages innovative technology."
Do "Losing is just motivation for the rematch."
Don't "Experience the ultimate competitive outdoor entertainment ecosystem."

Headline Formula

The default headline structure is: [Action Verb] + [Emotional Outcome]

  • Do: "Light Up Your Backyard"
  • Don't: "Purchase Our Premium LED Game Set"
  • Do: "Throw Down After Dark"
  • Don't: "Innovative Outdoor Entertainment Solution"
