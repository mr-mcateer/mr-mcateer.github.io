Digital Expansion & Exposure

Mike,

Now that the technological foundation of Joe's Salmon Lodge is modernized and optimized for aggressive conversion, the next step is driving highly qualified traffic to the site. The website acts as the net, but the content strategy operates as the bait.

Below is a curated list of high-leverage content adaptations and minor distribution tweaks designed to organically increase your lodge’s exposure across social media and search engines.

1. The "Trophy & Experience" Shot List (For 2026)

The modern luxury angler is buying the entire experience, not just the fish. Focus your camera and drone on capturing these specific, high-converting angles:

  • The Horizon Hook: Wide-angle, cinematic shots of a bent rod against the pristine backdrop of Hakai Pass. Show the isolation and the wilderness, not just the boat deck.
  • The Culinary Close-Up: High-resolution, well-lit photos of the West Coast buffet—steaks on the grill, fresh sashimi, or the morning breakfast spread. Food drives emotional bookings.
  • The Social Proof: Candid moments of camaraderie. Groups laughing at the bar, ringing the bell, and multi-generational families sharing a moment. Show the hospitality.
  • The "Short-Form" Video Action: 5-10 second vertical video clips (Reels/TikTok/Shorts) of fish breaking the surface, whales breaching, or eagles diving. Unedited, raw action performs infinitely better than over-produced commercials.

2. High-Impact Organic Positioning (SEO & Local)

We've handled the technical SEO (speed, semantic code, zero layout shift). Here is how you capture the "Zero-Click" audience using content:

  • Google Business Profile (GBP) Overhaul: Ensure your GBP is completely updated with the new site link. Upload 20+ of the new high-resolution photos directly to Google Business. Add a Q&A section there mirroring our FAQ. Google heavily prioritizes active, media-rich profiles in local searches.
  • Targeted Answering (Blog Strategy): Post 1-2 articles per month specifically answering questions anglers google: "Best Time to Fish Hakai Pass", "What Gear is provided on a BC Guided Trip?", or "How to Fly to the Central Coast." You become the factual authority.
  • Email Reactivation: Utilize your "Decade Club" list. Send a visually striking email announcing the newly updated website, 2026 open dates (linking directly to the new Calendar section), and a highlight reel of 2025's best catches.
