The goal of this redesign wasn’t just to make the lodge look better on a screen—it was to hand you a mechanical advantage over Hakai Lodge and Ole's Hakai Pass. Here is exactly what we solved and how it gives you the absolute edge in the pass for the 2026 season:

1. Eliminating the "Contact Us" Friction

The old industry standard relies on hiding prices and making busy anglers fill out inquiry forms just to find out if a weekend is open. We stripped that away. By wiring a Live Google Calendar directly into your Rates section, potential clients can immediately confirm your availability. 2. Mobile Supremacy & Technical SEO

Google heavily penalizes slow-loading websites, especially on smartphones. Your new site architecture is completely statically compiled. This means it loads instantaneously, doesn't bounce violently while scrolling, and never forces mobile users to "pinch and zoom" to read about the 17ft Boston Whalers. Google's search crawlers recognize this technical superiority, which will inherently force Joe's to climb higher in organic search results above older, clunky competitor sites.

3. The Aesthetic Authority

We purged the blurry compressed thumbnail photos from the old scrapers. Your gallery is now fueled by the raw, high-resolution original images. We stripped out the generic web colors and re-engineered the theme utilizing your precise Deep Hook Red (Hex: #A72F2A) and Nautical Blue (Hex: #2B5672), locking in your authentic vector typography and SVG logo to retain the legendary brand recognition your "Decade Club" anglers already trust.
