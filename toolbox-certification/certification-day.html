Toolbox Certification Day

Mr. McAteer -- Automotive Manufacturing -- Crescent Valley HS
 
 
 
 
Objective: Inventory, organize, and certify your toolbox. Every tool needed for engine work must be present, functional, and in the right place. This is graded.
Seniors (Abe, Zeph, Lincoln, Colton): You are auditing the teacher tool cabinet using this same process. Between your own audit, float to student boxes to help verify audit accuracy and answer questions after students have checked the reference materials.
Scan to open the Tool Identifier on your phone.
Search by name or description to ID unknown tools.

Procedure

1
Empty the toolbox completely. Lay every item on your bench. Group by type: sockets, wrenches, screwdrivers, pliers, specialty.
2
Audit each tool against the Master Tool List. Check the laminated list inside the lid. For each of the 20 required tools: Is it present? Rate condition 1-5. Mark action: K, R, or M.
3
Pull extras. Any tool NOT on the Master List goes to the Orphan Bin on the front bench. Log it on your Extras Table. To identify unknowns: (1) check the Mystery Tools reference on page 2, (2) use the Tool Identifier on your phone, (3) then ask a senior if you are still stuck.
4
Clean and organize. Wipe every tool. Arrange drawers: Top = sockets + ratchet + extensions. Middle = wrenches. Bottom = screwdrivers, pliers, specialty.
5
Get certified. Bring your completed audit form to Mr. McAteer. He spot-checks 3 items. If they match your form, you pass.
Condition 5 = New 4 = Good 3 = Worn 2 = Damaged 1 = Replace
Action K = Keep R = Replace M = Missing

What Is This Thing?

Common shop tools that are NOT part of the Predator 212 kit.

Found a tool not on your Master List? Check here first.

Correctly ID one by name + function = +2 bonus points
Tool Looks Like What It Does Route To
Torque wrench Ratchet with a dial, scale, or click mechanism on the handle Sets exact bolt tightness (ft-lb or in-lb). Used for final assembly only. Precision cart
Spark plug socket Deep socket with a rubber insert inside Grips the spark plug without cracking the ceramic insulator Shared shelf
Torx / star bits Star-shaped tips, usually in a folding set For automotive electronics fasteners. Not used on Predator 212. Instructor cabinet
Snap ring pliers Pliers with small pointed tips that bend in or out Removes retaining clips from shafts and bores Instructor cabinet
Impact driver (manual) Thick, heavy screwdriver body Strike with a hammer to free seized screws Instructor cabinet
Brass drift punch Solid brass rod, tapered tip Drives parts out without marring or damaging surfaces Shared shelf
Oil filter wrench Metal band or cap-grip tool Removes spin-on oil filters. Pred 212 has none -- this is a stray. Vehicle area
Harmonic balancer puller Heavy disc with center bolt and arms Removes press-fit pulleys. From the vehicle side of the shop. Vehicle area
Thread pitch gauge Fan of toothed metal blades Match blade teeth to bolt threads for thread ID Shared shelf
Gasket scraper Flat blade, looks like a putty knife Cleans gasket material from mating surfaces. Actually useful. KEEP if good
Tap and die set Cylindrical cutters in a hinged case Cuts or repairs threads. Belongs in the metals shop. Metals shop
Vacuum gauge Round dial with a rubber hose Measures intake vacuum on running engines Instructor cabinet
Bearing puller Spider arms with center bolt Pulls bearings off shafts. From vehicle work. Vehicle area
