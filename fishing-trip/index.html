Trip Overview

The Itinerary

Six days from Idaho, Oregon, and Utah to one of the most productive salmon fisheries on earth.

  • Sat, Aug 15
    Fly to Vancouver (YVR)
    5 from Idaho Falls (IDA), 1 from Eugene (EUG), 1 from Boise (BOI), 1 from Salt Lake City (SLC). Arrive afternoon. Overnight at hotel near YVR.
  • Sun, Aug 16
    Vancouver to Joe's Salmon Lodge
    Fly Pacific Coastal Airlines from YVR South Terminal to Bella Bella (ZEL). Water taxi to the lodge. Orientation and first evening at the lodge.
  • Mon - Thu
    Aug 17 - 20: Fish Hakai Pass
    Four full days on the water in Boston Whalers. Chinook, Coho, Halibut, Lingcod. Self-guided with GPS, radio, and daily tips from lodge staff.
  • Fri, Aug 21
    Final fishing day and return to Vancouver
    Morning fish, then charter flight back to YVR. Fish boxes travel with you. Overnight at hotel (fish freezer storage).
  • Sat, Aug 22
    Fly home
    Morning flights back to IDA, EUG, BOI, and SLC with your frozen salmon.

What's included in your lodge package

Joe's Salmon Lodge package includes:

  • Charter flights: YVR to Bella Bella and return
  • Water taxi transfer to and from the lodge
  • Family-style room (4 per room) with couch, TV, stocked fridge, private half-bath, heaters, WiFi, USB
  • All meals: breakfast buffet, packed lunch, dinner buffet, beverages
  • 17-foot Boston Whaler with GPS, fish finder, VHF radio (one per pair)
  • All fishing gear: rods, reels, tackle, bait, coolers
  • Professional fish processing: filleted, vacuum sealed, flash frozen, boxed
Not included
Bar tab, gratuities, fishing license, and flights to/from Vancouver.

Flight Synchronization

5 from Idaho Falls (IDA) + 1 from Eugene (EUG) + 1 from Boise (BOI) + 1 from Salt Lake City (SLC). All groups must land at YVR within ~2 hours of each other on Aug 15.

Recommended

The Airline Loyalty Hedge

If anyone in the group holds a Delta SkyMiles Amex, United Explorer, or Alaska Airlines Visa, use that card for the major airline legs (home airport to/from YVR). The charter to the lodge has no baggage fees. Waiving checked bag fees on the home legs for 8 people bringing 50lb fish coolers adds up to significant group savings.

Alternative

Absolute Lowest Fare

Booking the cheapest option on Expedia or Kayak across mixed carriers. Trade-off: you will face heavy checked bag and oversize fees at the counter on the way home for the fish boxes, likely wiping out any upfront ticket savings.

Layover rules
Minimum 1 hour 15 minutes for any connection. Stick to major hubs: SEA, SLC, or DEN. Do not book a 45-minute layover.

Self-Guided Fishing

This is not a guided charter
Joe's Salmon Lodge is a self-guided operation. Each pair of fishermen shares a 17-foot Boston Whaler with 60hp outboard and explores independently. The boat comes with a pre-programmed GPS, fish finder, VHF radio, and all safety equipment. You receive an orientation on arrival covering fishing areas, safety procedures, and equipment. A dinner placemat serves as your primary fishing area map. Staff provide tips and advice, but you run your own day on the water.

Return Logistics

Getting Your Fish Home

Your catch is professionally processed at the lodge. Here is how it gets from Hakai Pass to your home freezer.

🧋

Lodge Fish Processing

Joe's staff fillets, vacuum seals, flash freezes, and boxes all your fish. The boxes fly with you on the charter flight back to YVR. Your fish arrives in perfect condition.

❄️

Hotel Fish Storage

River Rock Casino Resort offers complimentary fish freezer storage for guests. Book with promo code "Fishing" or call 604-247-8900. Fresh ice available for repackaging.

🛂

Crossing the Border

Declare all fish at US Customs. Keep fish in original vacuum-sealed packaging. Retain your BC fishing license as proof of legal catch. Know your DFO possession limits.

Planner Note: Fish Box Airline Fees
The charter flight from the lodge to YVR has no baggage fees. Each fish box counts as a checked bag on your major airline leg home (YVR to IDA/EUG/BOI/SLC). Expect 50 lbs per box. If you used the airline loyalty card strategy, checked bag fees may be waived. Otherwise, check your airline's current baggage rates. This is where the loyalty card strategy pays for itself.

Documents & Regulations

What You Need

Required documents and fishing regulations for US citizens traveling to BC.

Document Details Action
Passport Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date (after Feb 2027). Required for entry into Canada. Verify expiration date now
BC Fishing License Non-resident tidal waters license required. Basic license + salmon stamp. Available for 1-day, 3-day, 5-day, or annual terms. Purchase online before the trip
Flight Insurance BC private insurance covers Pacific Coastal regional flights. Consider additional flight insurance for your major airline legs. Review when booking flights
Flight Confirmations Print copies of all flight itineraries: home to YVR, Pacific Coastal to Bella Bella, and return flights. Print before departure
Customs Declaration Declare all fish when re-entering the US. Personal consumption amounts are generally allowed. Keep your fishing license as proof. Be prepared at the border
DFO Area 7/107 Regulations
Hakai Pass falls under DFO Fisheries Area 7 (subareas 7 and 107). Catch limits are subject to in-season changes via DFO Fishery Notices. As of the last check, halibut retention was closed in these areas. Monitor the DFO website closer to your travel date for current regulations. The lodge staff will also brief you on arrival.
