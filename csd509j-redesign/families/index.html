Enrollment & Registration

Everything you need to register your child in CSD 509J

Corvallis School District welcomes new families throughout the year. To enroll your child, visit your neighborhood school or the District Office at 1555 SW 35th Street. Our enrollment team is available to walk you through the process and answer any questions.

Open Enrollment for 2026-27 Open enrollment for the 2026-27 school year is now underway. Families interested in requesting a school outside their attendance boundary may submit an open enrollment application. Contact your school or the District Office for details and timelines.

Required Documents

Please bring the following when registering a new student:

  • Birth certificate or proof of age
  • Proof of residence (utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement)
  • Immunization records (Oregon state requirements)
  • Previous school records or transcripts (if transferring)
  • Custody documentation (if applicable)

Transportation

Bus routes, real-time tracking, and safe travel to school

My Ride K-12 Bus Tracking

CSD 509J offers real-time bus tracking through the My Ride K-12 app. Download the free app on your smartphone to see exactly where your child's bus is, receive notifications about delays, and view estimated arrival times at your stop.

Download the App My Ride K-12 is available on both iOS and Android. Search "My Ride K-12" in your app store and select Corvallis School District to get started.

Bus Routes & Information

Bus route assignments are based on your home address and the school your child attends. Route information is available each August before the school year begins and is updated as needed throughout the year.

For questions about routes, stop locations, or eligibility, contact the Student Transportation department:

Phone: (541) 757-5838

Walking & Biking Safety

CSD 509J encourages safe walking and biking to school. Many of our schools participate in Safe Routes to School programs. Talk with your child about safe pedestrian and bicycle practices, including using crosswalks, wearing helmets, and following traffic signals. Contact your school for information about designated walk and bike routes in your neighborhood.

Food & Nutrition

Healthy meals for every student, every day

Corvallis School District serves breakfast and lunch daily at all schools. Our Food & Nutrition Services team prepares fresh, balanced meals that meet USDA nutritional guidelines. Weekly menus are posted online and include options for students with dietary restrictions.

Free & Reduced-Price Meals Families who qualify may receive free or reduced-price breakfast and lunch for their children. Applications are available year-round online or at your school office. Even if you are unsure whether you qualify, we encourage you to apply. Eligibility is based on household size and income, and the application process is confidential.

School Safety

Keeping our students and staff safe is our highest priority

Corvallis School District maintains comprehensive safety plans at every school. Our staff regularly trains on emergency procedures, and we coordinate closely with local law enforcement and emergency responders to ensure the safety of our school communities.

Emergency Procedures

Each school has detailed plans for a range of emergency scenarios, including fire, earthquake, lockdown, and severe weather. Schools conduct regular drills throughout the year so students and staff are prepared. In the event of an emergency, families will be notified through our mass communication system by phone, email, and text.

Reunification Procedures

If an emergency requires students to be relocated, CSD 509J follows established reunification procedures. Parents and guardians will be directed to a designated reunification site where they can pick up their children. Bring a valid photo ID. Details are shared at the beginning of each school year and during emergencies.

SafeOregon Tip Line SafeOregon is a statewide school safety tip line. Students, parents, and community members can report safety concerns 24/7 by calling or texting 1-844-472-3367 or by submitting a tip online at safeoregon.com. All tips are reviewed and forwarded to the appropriate school and law enforcement for follow-up.

School Safety Plans

Every CSD 509J school has a site-specific safety plan developed in coordination with the district's Safety & Risk Management department and local emergency services. These plans are reviewed and updated annually. If you have questions about your school's safety procedures, contact your school's main office or the district's Safety & Risk Management department.

Special Education & Multilingual Services

Supporting every learner with individualized resources and multilingual access

Special Education

CSD 509J provides a full continuum of special education services for students with disabilities from birth through age 21. Our special education team works collaboratively with families to develop Individualized Education Programs (IEPs) and 504 plans tailored to each student's unique needs.

If you have concerns about your child's learning or development, contact your child's teacher or school to request an evaluation. The district's Student Growth & Experience department oversees all special education services.

IEP & 504 Plans An IEP outlines specialized instruction and related services for students who qualify under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). A 504 plan provides accommodations for students with disabilities who need support to access the general education curriculum. Both are developed with parent involvement.

Multilingual Services

Our students and families speak 74 languages across the district. CSD 509J is committed to ensuring that all families, regardless of language, can fully participate in their child's education.

The district's multilingual family liaisons provide interpretation and translation services, help families navigate school systems, and connect families with community resources. Liaisons are available in multiple languages including Spanish, Arabic, Mandarin, and many others.

Language Support Need help communicating with your child's school? Contact the Multilingual Services office to request an interpreter for conferences, meetings, or phone calls. Translated documents are also available for key enrollment and school communications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions from CSD 509J families

How do I enroll my child?

To enroll a new student, visit your neighborhood school or the District Office at 1555 SW 35th Street, Corvallis, OR 97333. Bring a birth certificate or proof of age, proof of residence, and immunization records. Staff will guide you through the registration process. You can also call (541) 757-5811 for assistance.

What are school hours?

School hours vary by level. Approximate hours are:

  • Elementary Schools: 8:00 AM – 2:30 PM
  • Middle Schools: 7:45 AM – 2:15 PM
  • High Schools: 8:00 AM – 3:00 PM

Hours may vary by school. Check your individual school's page or contact the school office for exact start and end times.

How do I find my child's bus route?

Download the free My Ride K-12 app (available on iOS and Android) and select Corvallis School District. The app shows your child's assigned route, stop locations, and real-time bus tracking. You can also contact the Student Transportation department at (541) 757-5838 for route information.

Who do I contact about special education?

For questions about special education evaluations, IEPs, 504 plans, or related services, start by speaking with your child's teacher or school counselor. You can also contact the district's Student Growth & Experience department at (541) 757-5811 for guidance and support.

How do I apply for free or reduced-price meals?

Applications for the free and reduced-price meal program are available at your school office or online. Eligibility is based on household size and income, and the application process is confidential. For questions or help with your application, contact Food & Nutrition Services at (541) 757-5841.
