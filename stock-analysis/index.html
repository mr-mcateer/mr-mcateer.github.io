The numbers on this page come from an AI research pipeline that searches public internet sources, reads financial filings, and synthesizes what it finds. That process has structural blind spots no amount of source volume can fix. Sources are tier-1 only: SEC EDGAR, earnings transcripts, Fed / FOMC, Reuters, Bloomberg, WSJ, FT, AP, BBC, Economist, Nikkei, CSIS, CFR, Brookings, Chatham House, Atlantic Council, IISS, PIIE, ECB, and .gov docket pages.

What the model CAN see

  • ● Public earnings data, SEC filings, transcripts
  • ● Published analyst targets and consensus ratings
  • ● Tier-1 news reporting
  • ● Historical price data and technical levels
  • ● Public court filings and regulatory actions
  • ● Macro indicators (VIX, yields, crude, CPI)
  • ● CBOE delayed-quote option chains (15-minute lag)

What the model CANNOT see

  • ● Insider sentiment, boardroom dynamics, private negotiations
  • ● Real-time order flow, dark pool activity, options gamma
  • ● Classified geopolitical intelligence
  • ● Unreported supply chain disruptions
  • ● Undisclosed legal settlements or pending lawsuits
  • ● Central bank decisions before they're announced
  • ● Black swan events (by definition, unpredictable)
