Macro Context

Why These Holdings Now

The structural forces behind both positions.

Memory Supercycle

DRAM / HBM / DDR5
$100B HBM TAM by 2028
+171% DRAM pricing YoY

AI training and inference have created insatiable demand for high-bandwidth memory. HBM supply is contracted years in advance, DDR5 spot prices have quadrupled since September 2025, and the structural shortage persists through at least 2027.

HBM4 ramps Q2 CY2026 at significantly higher margins. The $100B TAM arrives two full years ahead of prior forecasts. This is the core thesis for holding MU.

AI Capex Boom

Hyperscaler Infrastructure
$125B META 2026 capex (midpoint)
$200B+ Combined hyperscaler spend

Every major hyperscaler is in an AI infrastructure arms race. Meta's $115-135B spend in 2026 directly benefits memory suppliers like Micron while creating execution risk for Meta itself.

The key tension: this capex simultaneously supports the MU bull case (demand driver) and the META bear case (uncertain returns). These holdings are structurally linked.
